About Batsheva Baraban

Batsheva Baraban is the founder of Spiralead, delivering lectures, workshops, and trainings that help teams, executives, and organizations navigate complexity, culture, and leadership with clarity and impact.

With over a decade of experience leading cross-cultural teams, scaling education startups, and designing global leadership programs, Batsheva brings practical frameworks rooted in real-world challenges. Her sessions combine insight, strategy, and interactive reflection to equip participants with tools they can immediately apply.

Signature Topics

  • Negotiation as Navigation – mastering negotiation as a leadership skill
  • Bridging Cultural Gaps – working effectively across cultures and borders
  • Women's Leadership Empowerment – amplifying voice, presence, and impact

Batsheva holds a Master's in International Education Policy from Harvard University, a BA in Political Science and Philosophy (Summa Cum Laude) and a coaching license. French-Israeli, she speaks Hebrew, English, and French fluently.

Bridging the Gap: Cultural Intelligence for Global Teams

1 hour experiential lecture

An experiential lecture for global teams working across Israel and the U.S. Turn cultural friction into trust, clarity, and stronger performance.

The Challenge

Global teams fail less because of strategy and more because of unspoken cultural misunderstandings: directness mistaken for rudeness, diplomacy read as avoidance, speed confused with chaos, and structure seen as rigidity.

What Participants Will Gain

  • Decode hidden cultural assumptions in communication, feedback, decision-making, and conflict
  • Understand why misunderstandings occur between Israeli and U.S.-based teams
  • Learn how to adapt without losing authenticity
  • Turn cultural friction into trust, clarity, and stronger performance

Ideal For

  • Global and hybrid teams
  • Israeli companies working with U.S. counterparts
  • Managers leading multicultural teams
  • HR leaders seeking high-impact, non-generic learning experiences

Outcome: Clearer communication, reduced friction, and more effective collaboration across borders.

Negotiation as Navigation

1.5 hour interactive workshop

A practical, experiential workshop for everyday negotiations. Harvard Kennedy School frameworks adapted to Israeli workplace culture.

Why This Workshop

Negotiation is no longer limited to sales or procurement. Employees negotiate daily: priorities, salaries, deadlines, resources, hybrid work, boundaries, and even personal commitments that affect performance.

Yet most people approach negotiation intuitively, emotionally, or defensively—often escalating conflict instead of creating movement.

The Core Approach

Instead of teaching negotiation as persuasion or "winning", the workshop reframes it as navigation: How do you move forward when interests clash, emotions run high, or power is uneven?

Participants Learn To

  • Separate people from problems
  • Identify real interests beneath positions
  • Communicate under pressure
  • Generate options beyond "yes/no"
  • Know when to agree and when to walk away

What Participants Experience

  • A live negotiation simulation that reveals common blind spots
  • Guided reflection on emotions, perception, and communication
  • A structured listening exercise that replaces debate with understanding
  • Practical tools for immediate application

Key Concepts Covered

  • People vs. Problem: Managing emotions without personalizing conflict
  • Interests vs. Positions: Understanding the "why" behind demands
  • Option Generation: Creating value before making trade-offs
  • Objective Criteria: Making fair decisions without power struggles
  • BATNA: Knowing your alternatives and negotiation power

Outcome: More confident, clear, and effective in difficult conversations—at work and beyond.
